## 🧪 QC Mindset

<details open>
<summary><strong>What should you check before shipping?</strong></summary>

- Compare warehouse photos against listing photos.
- Request measurements when sizing matters.
- Check embroidery, stitching, shape, color, tags, and hardware.
- Think about parcel weight before adding bulky shoes or jackets.
- Do not approve international shipping just because the item arrived at the warehouse.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>When should you return or exchange?</strong></summary>

Return or exchange when you see major sizing mismatches, obvious damage, wrong color, poor print placement, severe shape issues, missing details, or a seller substitution that changes what you thought you ordered.

</details>

## ❓ FAQ

<details>
<summary><strong>Is this only a spreadsheet?</strong></summary>

No. It works like a spreadsheet-style discovery hub plus a buyer guide library. You can browse categories, read QC notes, and use the guide pages to make better decisions before shipping.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Are QC photos really necessary?</strong></summary>

Yes. QC photos are one of the most important checkpoints in agent shopping. They help you catch sizing, shape, stitching, color, and detail problems before international shipping.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Which categories are best for beginners?</strong></summary>

Accessories, t-shirts, and some hoodies are usually easier to evaluate. Shoes and jackets can be more sensitive because sizing, shape, weight, and packaging matter more.

</details>
